A **PID Controller** (Proportional-Integral-Derivative) continuously calculates an error value and applies corrections using three distinct gains:

  • P (Proportional Gain): Reacts to the present error. A higher **Kp** increases responsiveness but may cause overshoot.
  • I (Integral Gain): Accumulates past errors to eliminate steady-state error. A high **Ki** can reduce error but may cause instability.
  • D (Derivative Gain): Predicts future errors based on the error rate of change. A high **Kd** dampens oscillations but may slow response.

The right balance of these **gains** ensures smooth, accurate control. Learn more about PID controllers.
