About Me

I am Rob Rainer, a Senior Technology Engineer with over 15 years of experience at Brookhaven National Laboratory. My expertise spans EPICS-based control systems, accelerator operations, AI/ML integration, and system optimization.

I hold a Bachelor of Science in Physics and a Master of Science in Engineering, equipping me with a robust foundation in both theoretical physics and practical engineering. This academic and professional background has allowed me to contribute meaningfully to the advancement of accelerator operations and control systems engineering.

My responsibilities include managing critical accelerator systems, transitioning infrastructure to a secure virtualization model, and modernizing operations through advanced tools and technologies. I have also been actively involved in applying AI/ML techniques to accelerator research and automation processes. My work has also involved collaborations with Oak Ridge National Laboratory (ORNL) and Argonne National Laboratory, where I contributed to multidisciplinary projects. Additionally, I have provided consulting and IT services to private organizations, leveraging my expertise across industries.

Beyond my technical contributions, I am passionate about mentoring junior engineers and interns, fostering a collaborative and innovative environment for the next generation of professionals.

My Critical Systems (EPICS-based)

As a Senior Technology Engineer, I manage several critical systems essential to the smooth operation of NSLS-II:

  • Utilities Systems: Supporting accelerator and facility performance with reliable operations.
  • Vacuum Systems: Maintaining beamline and accelerator integrity through optimized vacuum systems.
  • EPS (Equipment Protection System): Safeguarding equipment and personnel with robust protection systems.
  • PPS (Personnel Protection System): Ensuring safety across NSLS-II with secure and efficient systems.
  • Timing System: Developing and maintaining accelerator systems for precise timing and synchronization.
  • Operations IOCs and GUIs: Creating intuitive GUIs and tailored IOCs for streamlined operations.

My Professional Journey

My journey in science and engineering spans diverse fields, from advanced materials research to leading-edge accelerator operations.

I began as a Laboratory Supervisor at West Chester University of Pennsylvania, using advanced electron microscopy techniques for my undergraduate thesis on materials characterization. After earning my Bachelor of Science in Physics in 2007, I joined a Materials R&D lab, conducting research on solid oxide fuel cells (SOFCs) and photovoltaics to advance alternative energy technologies.

In 2010, I transitioned to Brookhaven National Laboratory (BNL), starting at NSLS-I as an operations coordinator and accelerator operator. During this time, I earned my Master of Science in Engineering. At NSLS-I, I supported beamline operations and coordinated experiments. With the commissioning of NSLS-II, I contributed to its First Light success by developing EPICS-based GUIs, commissioning the injector and storage ring, and establishing the operations center infrastructure.

I joined the NSLS-II Accelerator Controls Group in 2020 and took ownership of several critical systems. I integrated modern cybersecurity practices, transitioned infrastructure to a virtualization model, and applied AI/ML techniques to cutting-edge accelerator research. My system administration and control integration work helped ensure the reliability and optimization of NSLS-II’s critical infrastructure.

Earlier this year, I joined Osprey DCS, where I now work on projects for multiple national laboratories — including continuing efforts at BNL as well as controls integration and EPICS development at Lawrence Berkeley National Laboratory (LBNL). My work spans the design, deployment, and modernization of control systems and instrumentation infrastructure across diverse environments.
